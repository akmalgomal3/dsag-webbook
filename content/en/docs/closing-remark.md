## What You Have Learned

This book was designed with a singular purpose: to transform how you think about problems. Not by memorizing algorithms, but by understanding the **principles** that make them work:

- **Trade-offs are everything.** There is no best data structure or algorithm: only the right one for your specific constraints. Speed versus space. Simplicity versus optimality. Determinism versus probability.
- **Hardware matters.** Cache lines, memory hierarchies, and garbage collection pressure often dominate theoretical Big-O. An O(n) algorithm can lose to O(n log n) when the constants and cache behavior differ.
- **Go is pragmatic.** The language rewards simplicity, composition, and explicitness. The standard library (`container/heap`, `sort`, `sync`) solves many problems well: know when to use it and when to build your own.
- **History informs the future.** The algorithms we use today were forged over millennia of human problem-solving. Understanding their origins prevents reinventing wheels and reveals timeless patterns.
